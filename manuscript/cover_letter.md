We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled **"Digital Exposome Aging Index (DEAI): A Multi-Domain Composite Score for Predicting Accelerated Biological Ageing in Population Settings"** for consideration as an original research article.

**Why this paper matters:** The global burden of accelerated biological ageing falls disproportionately on low- and middle-income countries (LMICs) such as India, where individuals face compounded exposome adversity — extreme air pollution, heat stress, tobacco use, nutritional deficits, and poverty. Yet existing biological ageing clocks (Horvath, PhenoAge, GrimAge) require individual molecular measurements unavailable at population scale in these settings. We present the first multi-domain Digital Exposome Aging Index (DEAI) designed from the ground up for population data, validated using an explainable AI framework, and packaged as a fully reproducible open-science pipeline.

**Novelty statement:**
1. First DEAI specifically incorporating LMIC-relevant exposures (PM2.5, heat, diet diversity, tobacco, SES) into a validated composite ageing score.
2. Demonstrates that DEAI outperforms chronological age alone for prediction of frailty, multimorbidity, and disability.
3. Explainable AI (SHAP) identifies modifiable vs. non-modifiable contributors, directly supporting preventive intervention targeting.
4. Biological triangulation with independent GEO transcriptomics provides molecular plausibility for DEAI domains.
5. Fully reproducible pipeline (Python, MIT license) ready for direct application to LASI or DHS microdata.

**Target audience fit:** This manuscript is directly relevant to *Nature Aging*'s scope of "biological, medical, social and public health" dimensions of ageing, particularly the journal's focus on translatable population-level insights. The open-data methodology and LMIC focus also align with the journal's commitment to global research equity.
