Subject: A note of thanks, and an update following your decision on IJMR_1754_2026

Dear Dr Gupta,

I am writing with regard to manuscript IJMR_1754_2026 ("Biological ageing vulnerability among
adults aged 45 years and older in India…"), which the journal declined in June 2026 after detailed
external review.

I want first to thank you and the three reviewers sincerely. The critique was rigorous and, in
retrospect, correct on its central point: the "integrated vulnerability index" we had constructed
was circular — it combined social and functional measures and then used social variables to predict
that same composite — and it had not been validated against anything external to itself. Rather than
contest those comments, I treated them as a specification for what the study should have been.

The work was therefore rebuilt from the ground up. The composite index was abandoned. In its place,
the redesigned study uses only directly measured biomarkers, defines abnormality by established
external clinical thresholds (ADA, WHO, CDC/AHA), removes every social and self-reported variable
from the outcome, and validates the biological signal against an outcome — past-year hospitalisation —
that is not part of the construct. The central finding is one the reviewers' logic pointed toward:
measured biomarkers reveal a large, socially patterned burden of **undiagnosed** disease that
self-report and simple indicators cannot see — about half of biochemical diabetes and three-fifths of
measured hypertension in older Indians are undiagnosed, concentrated in rural, less-educated and poorer
groups, alongside a contrasting cardiometabolic burden among the urban and affluent.

I am glad to report that this redesigned study has now been accepted for publication in
*Discover Aging* (Springer Nature) [add citation/DOI on acceptance]. I share this not to revisit the
earlier decision, which I accept, but to close the loop: the reviewers' criticism directly produced a
stronger, more honest, and more policy-relevant paper than the one originally submitted, and I am
grateful for it.

I would be glad to submit future work to IJMR and hope the journal will consider it on its merits.

With respect and thanks,

Dr Siddalingaiah H S
Professor, Department of Community Medicine
Shridevi Institute of Medical Sciences and Research Hospital, Karnataka, India
