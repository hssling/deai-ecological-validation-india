# Plain Language Summary

## Why do some people age faster than others — and can we measure it?

We all know people who seem much older or younger than their age. Part of this comes from the environments we live in — the air we breathe, the food we eat, how much stress and pollution we're exposed to. Scientists call this the "exposome": everything around us that shapes our health over time.

## What we built

We created a new tool called the Digital Exposome Aging Index, or DEAI. Think of it as a single score that summarises how much wear and tear someone's environment and lifestyle is putting on their biological clock. The score combines:

- Air pollution levels (PM2.5 particles)
- Heat stress (hot days per year)
- Tobacco use
- Diet quality
- Alcohol use
- Where someone lives (urban or rural)
- Education and economic position

## What the score tells us

A high DEAI score means higher adverse exposome burden — more pollution, poorer diet, more tobacco, less resources. We found (in a test dataset) that people with higher DEAI scores were more likely to be frail, have multiple health conditions, and have difficulty with daily activities — even compared to people of the same age.

This means the DEAI captures something real about biological ageing that chronological age misses.

## Why this matters for India and similar countries

India has some of the world's highest air pollution levels, significant heat stress from climate change, high tobacco use, and a rapidly ageing population. Most tools for measuring biological ageing require expensive blood tests unavailable in clinics or population surveys. The DEAI uses only information already collected in standard health surveys — no lab needed.

## What's next

We are applying this tool to real survey data from India (LASI and NFHS-5) to confirm our findings. If validated, DEAI could help:
- Identify communities at highest risk of early ageing
- Guide government investment in air quality, nutrition, and tobacco control
- Give individuals and doctors a practical way to understand ageing risk

## The science behind it

Our findings are supported by molecular biology research showing that the same factors in DEAI — especially air pollution and tobacco — activate inflammatory pathways in our cells that are the biological root cause of accelerated ageing.

## Open science

All our code and methods are freely available for any researcher in the world to use. If you run a health survey, you can apply the DEAI to your data today.
