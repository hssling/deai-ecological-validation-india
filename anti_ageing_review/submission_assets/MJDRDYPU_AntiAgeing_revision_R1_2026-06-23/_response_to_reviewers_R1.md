**Point-by-Point Response to Reviewers**

**Manuscript:** mjdrdypu_451_26 — "Can Ageing Be Slowed or Reversed? A Reproducible Evidence Map and Credibility Ranking of Anti-Ageing and Age-Reversal Interventions"

**Journal:** Medical Journal of Dr. D.Y. Patil Vidyapeeth (MJDRDYPU)

**Date:** 23 June 2026

I thank the editor and the reviewer for their careful reading. Responses are given in table form below. Section and page references point to the revised manuscript.

**A preliminary clarification.** This manuscript is a reproducible bibliographic evidence map of anti-ageing intervention classes. It does not enrol patients, does not define diabetic or non-diabetic groups, does not administer cognitive instruments such as the MMSE, and holds no individual-level medication or socioeconomic data. Several reviewer comments (Comments 1, 2, and 4) describe features of a patient-level comparative clinical cohort — diabetic versus non-diabetic groups, MMSE-based cognitive labelling, and individual polypharmacy assessment — that are not part of this study. I respectfully raise the possibility that these comments may have been intended for a different manuscript. Rather than set them aside, I have engaged the underlying geriatric principles each comment raises and shown how they are handled within the scope of an evidence map. I would of course welcome any further reviewer comments specific to this manuscript.
